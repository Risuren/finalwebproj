About the
University of Rizal System

Who We Are

The University of Rizal System (URS) is a premier public institution dedicated to fostering academic excellence, leadership, and community empowerment. As the leading university in the province of Rizal, URS operates ten campuses strategically located across the province, offering a wide range of programs from Kindergarten to Graduate School. We are committed to shaping upright and competent graduates who are equipped to contribute to national development and global progress.

Our History

1944

Founding of Morong High School

Morong High School is established during the final years of World War II, providing young Filipinos in Rizal province with access to quality secondary education. Despite the challenges of the era, the school quickly became a vital institution for academic growth in the community.
1956

Establishment of Rizal National Agricultural School (RNAS)

Through Republic Act No. 1560, authored by Congressman Serafin Salvador, the Rizal National Agricultural School is created to promote agricultural education and rural development. This marks the beginning of Rizal’s journey toward specialized higher education.
1983

Growth of Technical and Polytechnic Education

Around the same period, Morong National Comprehensive School transitioned into Rizal Technological and Polytechnic Institute (RTPI) through Batas Pambansa 469. Continuing its advancement, RTPI became Rizal Polytechnic College (RPC) in 1995 by virtue of Republic Act 7933, strengthening its offerings in technical and polytechnic education. Similarly, in 1995, RCAT evolved into Rizal State College (RSC) through Republic Act 7858, further cementing the province's growing network of higher education institutions.
2001

A New Chapter: Birth of the University of Rizal System

On August 11, 2001, Republic Act 9157 officially merged Rizal State College, Rizal Polytechnic College, and the Rizal Technological University-Antipolo Annex to form the University of Rizal System (URS). This integration brought together distinct academic traditions and programs into one unified system, dedicated to serving the educational and developmental needs of Rizal and its neighboring areas.
2002

Tanay Designated as Main Campus

In 2002, the Commission on Higher Education, through Resolution No. 411-4004, identified Tanay Campus as the main campus of the University. This decision established Tanay as the heart of URS’s academic, administrative, and research activities.
